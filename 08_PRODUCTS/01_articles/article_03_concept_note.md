\# Article 03 – Concept Note



\## Tentative title

FNRR as a structural indicator of regional wind-energy irregularity and usable energy estimation under uncertainty



\## Scientific problem

In wind-energy assessment, the existence of energetic potential does not automatically imply the same degree of practical usability. Regions with strong variability, abrupt transitions, and predictive uncertainty may exhibit relevant differences between free energy potential and structurally usable energy. This motivates the definition of an interpretable indicator capable of quantifying regional non-regularity in a physically meaningful way.



\## Objective

To define and analyze FNRR as a structural indicator of regional wind-energy irregularity and to relate it to the estimation of usable wind energy under uncertainty.



\## Expected contribution

This article is expected to contribute:

\- the formal introduction of FNRR as an original structural indicator,

\- a scientific distinction between free energy and usable energy,

\- a regional interpretation of non-regularity in wind-energy behavior,

\- a bridge between physical interpretation, uncertainty, and decision-oriented modeling.
