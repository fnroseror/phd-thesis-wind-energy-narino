\# Patent Disclosure Note



\## Tentative title

System and method for estimating usable wind energy under regional non-regularity conditions using a structural indicator and hybrid physical-statistical modeling



\## Technical problem

Wind-energy assessment commonly focuses on gross energetic potential, but this does not necessarily reflect the same degree of practical usability. In regions with strong atmospheric variability, topographic effects, and predictive uncertainty, the difference between available free energy and structurally usable energy becomes relevant. Existing approaches may describe the resource, but they do not always provide an interpretable structural indicator that links variability, uncertainty, and operational usability.



\## Proposed technical solution

The proposed solution consists of a physical-statistical framework for estimating usable wind energy by:

\- calculating energy-related variables from meteorological observations,

\- characterizing structural non-regularity at the regional level,

\- defining an interpretable indicator associated with non-regularity,

\- linking this indicator to the distinction between free energy and usable energy,

\- supporting decision-oriented interpretation through hybrid modeling outputs.



\## Novelty logic

The novelty is associated with the integration of:

\- a physically meaningful distinction between free energy and usable energy,

\- a structural regional non-regularity indicator,

\- uncertainty-aware interpretation,

\- hybrid physical-statistical support for decision-oriented evaluation of wind-energy usability.
