# 04_RESULTS_COMPLETE

## Purpose

This folder contains the complete result and evidence layer associated with the doctoral thesis:

**“Estudio de la velocidad de viento e inclusión de parámetros físicos para la predicción de energía eléctrica producida por fuentes eólicas”.**

Its purpose is to organize, preserve, and document the computational outputs, graphical evidence, tabular evidence, uncertainty products, FNRR outputs, extended results, and energetic projection material generated by the doctoral workflow.

This folder should be interpreted as the main scientific evidence layer of the repository.

It does not replace the thesis document. Instead, it supports the thesis by preserving the organized outputs required for external academic review, scientific traceability, and computational reproducibility.

---

## 1. Scientific role of this folder

The doctoral thesis develops a physical–statistical and computational framework for the characterization, forecasting, uncertainty evaluation, and energetic interpretation of wind power potential in Nariño, Colombia.

The central physical target variable is **Wind Power Density (WPD)**:

```text
WPD = 0.5 · ρ · v³
```

where:

- `WPD` is Wind Power Density,
- `ρ` is air density,
- and `v` is wind speed.

Because WPD depends cubically on wind speed, the results must preserve physical, statistical, predictive, probabilistic, and energetic traceability.

For that reason, this folder integrates:

- physical characterization results,
- deterministic model-comparison results,
- probabilistic uncertainty outputs,
- FNRR outputs,
- energy-projection results,
- extended technical outputs,
- visual evidence,
- and tabular evidence.

## 3. Folder description

### `01_physical_characterization/`

Contains structured outputs associated with the physical–statistical characterization of wind behavior.

This section supports:

- descriptive statistics,
- Weibull fitting,
- Rayleigh comparison,
- temporal dependence through ACF and PACF,
- spectral structure through FFT,
- time–frequency behavior through Wavelet analysis,
- and supporting outputs associated with the physical interpretation of the wind regime.

Scientific role:

This folder supports the physical understanding of the atmospheric system before predictive modeling. It connects the observed meteorological records with the physical characterization developed in the thesis.

---

### `02_model_comparison/`

Contains outputs related to deterministic predictive performance.

This section supports the comparison of forecasting models applied to Wind Power Density and related variables.

It may include:

- model-performance summaries,
- RMSE,
- MAE,
- R²,
- Skill Score against persistence,
- horizon-based evaluation,
- and zone-based performance outputs.

Scientific role:

This folder supports the evaluation of whether the proposed modeling framework improves predictive capacity relative to baseline approaches, especially the persistence benchmark.

---

### `03_pi90_uncertainty/`

Contains outputs associated with probabilistic uncertainty assessment using PI90 intervals.

It may include:

- empirical coverage before calibration,
- empirical coverage after calibration,
- calibration factors,
- prediction-interval outputs,
- and uncertainty-support tables or objects.

Scientific role:

This folder supports the probabilistic component of the thesis. It allows the results to be interpreted not only as point predictions, but as predictions under uncertainty.

---

### `04_fnrr_outputs/`

Contains outputs associated with the **Factor de No Regularidad Regional (FNRR)**.

It may include:

- FNRR values by zone,
- structural irregularity summaries,
- energy-adjustment outputs,
- comparisons between free and usable energy,
- and supporting results for regional energetic interpretation.

Scientific role:

This folder supports one of the original contributions of the thesis: the use of a regional irregularity descriptor to distinguish between physically available energy and structurally usable energy.

---

### `05_energy_projection/`

Contains outputs associated with regional energy integration and projection.

It may include:

- annual free energy,
- annual usable energy,
- projected WPD-related outputs,
- energy scenarios up to 2028,
- and projection-support summaries.

Scientific role:

This folder connects the predictive framework with the regional energetic interpretation of the thesis. It supports the translation from atmospheric behavior to energy-potential analysis.

---

### `06_extended_results/`

Contains additional outputs required for technical support, traceability, and reproducibility.

It may include:

- extended metrics,
- auxiliary model outputs,
- additional horizons,
- residual-support outputs,
- supplementary results,
- and model-specific supporting material.

Scientific role:

This folder provides additional evidence that may not appear directly in the main thesis body but supports external review and reproducibility.

---

### `07_FIGURES/`

Contains visual evidence associated with the thesis.

The figures are organized by thesis chapter:

- `chapter_2/`: physical characterization and factor analysis,
- `chapter_3/`: predictive modeling and data-driven analysis,
- `chapter_4/`: hybrid physical–statistical integration, FNRR, uncertainty, and energy projection.

Scientific role:

This folder allows reviewers to inspect the graphical evidence that supports the interpretation of the doctoral results.

---

### `08_TABLES/`

Contains tabular evidence associated with the thesis.

The tables are organized by thesis chapter:

- `chapter_2/`: physical characterization and descriptive-inferential analysis,
- `chapter_3/`: forecasting models, metrics, and comparative performance,
- `chapter_4/`: FNRR, uncertainty, energy projection, and integrated results.

Scientific role:

This folder provides the structured numerical evidence supporting the written interpretation, model evaluation, and energetic conclusions of the thesis.

## 6. Interpretation criteria

The results contained in this folder should be interpreted according to the following principles:

### 6.1 Physical consistency

Results are interpreted from the behavior of the atmospheric system and from the physical meaning of Wind Power Density.

### 6.2 Zonal structure

Results are organized and interpreted according to the four analytical zones defined in the doctoral dataset.

### 6.3 Predictive evaluation

Model outputs must be read in relation to deterministic metrics, probabilistic intervals, and Skill Score against persistence.

### 6.4 Uncertainty awareness

PI90 outputs are part of the scientific interpretation, not an accessory result.

### 6.5 Energetic interpretation

Free energy, usable energy, FNRR, and projection outputs should be interpreted as part of the regional energy-potential framework.

### 6.6 Reproducibility

Results must remain traceable to metadata, code, tables, figures, appendices, and reproducibility files.
