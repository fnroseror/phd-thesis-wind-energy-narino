# Station–Zone Mapping

## Purpose

This document defines the spatial organization of the meteorological station network used in the doctoral thesis:

**“Estudio de la velocidad de viento e inclusión de parámetros físicos para la predicción de energía eléctrica producida por fuentes eólicas”.**

Its purpose is to document the correspondence between the observational stations and the four geographic zones used as the regional analysis structure of the thesis.

This file does not introduce a new zoning criterion. It documents the zonal structure used in the consolidated doctoral dataset and preserves the spatial traceability required to connect the observational data, the physical–statistical characterization, the predictive modeling framework, the uncertainty analysis, and the regional energetic interpretation.

---

## 1. General mapping principle

The doctoral research is based on approximately **8 million hourly meteorological records** corresponding to the period **2017–2022**, obtained from **16 IDEAM meteorological stations** and grouped into **four analytical zones** in the department of **Nariño, Colombia**.

The grouping by zone was adopted as the operational spatial structure of the thesis in order to support:

- regional physical interpretation,
- station-level traceability,
- consistency in the construction of zonal datasets,
- comparative analysis between regional regimes,
- predictive modeling at the zonal level,
- uncertainty evaluation,
- and energy-potential projection.

Within the thesis, the **zone** is therefore the operational spatial unit used to organize the atmospheric system.

## 10. Normalization criteria

The master station inventory preserves the source station names as they appear in the original records.

For repository consistency, this document distinguishes between:

- **Source station name:** original name as registered in the dataset.
- **Normalized station name:** harmonized name used for internal repository documentation, generally removing suffixes such as `- AUT`.

This distinction is important because it preserves traceability to the original data while maintaining consistency in the repository documentation.

No scientific conclusion should depend only on the normalized station name. The station code remains the primary traceability key.

---

## 11. Role of the zonal structure in the doctoral workflow

The zonal structure is used throughout the thesis and repository in the following stages:

### 11.1 Data organization

The station records are grouped by zone to construct regional datasets used in subsequent analysis.

### 11.2 Physical–statistical characterization

Each zone is analyzed as a regional atmospheric subsystem using descriptive, distributional, temporal, and spectral tools.

### 11.3 Predictive modeling

Forecasting models are trained, evaluated, and compared at the zonal level.

### 11.4 Uncertainty analysis

Prediction intervals and probabilistic evaluation are interpreted by zone.

### 11.5 Energy-potential interpretation

Wind Power Density, free energy, usable energy, FNRR, and energetic projections are interpreted according to the zonal structure.

---

## 12. Methodological caution

The zonal grouping should be interpreted as the validated operational structure of the doctoral dataset.

This document does not claim that the zones are official geographic, administrative, climatic, or topographic divisions. Their role is methodological and analytical: they organize the observational system into regional units that allow consistent physical–statistical modeling.

Any detailed geographic, altitudinal, or climatological interpretation should be supported by additional station metadata, maps, or territorial evidence when required.
