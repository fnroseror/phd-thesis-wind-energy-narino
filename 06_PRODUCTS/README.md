# 06_PRODUCTS

## Purpose

This folder contains the academic, technological, pedagogical, and applied products derived from the doctoral thesis:

**“Estudio de la velocidad de viento e inclusión de parámetros físicos para la predicción de energía eléctrica producida por fuentes eólicas”.**

The purpose of this section is to organize the products that emerge from the doctoral work beyond the dissertation itself, including articles, patent-oriented material, book development, dashboards, presentations, directed theses, and broader professorial or institutional projects.

This folder should be interpreted as the **projection layer** of the repository.

It is not part of the core evidentiary structure of the thesis. The core scientific support is contained in:

```text
01_THESIS/
02_DATA_METADATA/
03_CODE/
04_RESULTS_COMPLETE/
05_APPENDICES_SUPPORT/
07_REPRODUCIBILITY/
```

---

## 1. Scientific role of this folder

The doctoral thesis produced a physical–statistical and computational framework for the characterization, prediction, uncertainty quantification, and energetic interpretation of wind power potential in Nariño, Colombia.

The central doctoral framework includes:

- physical characterization of wind behavior,
- construction of Wind Power Density,
- predictive modeling,
- uncertainty quantification through PI90,
- FNRR as a regional irregularity descriptor,
- free energy and usable energy interpretation,
- and regional energy projection toward 2028.

The products in this folder are derived from that scientific development.

They may support future dissemination, publication, technological development, pedagogy, institutional projection, or social appropriation of knowledge.

## 4. Relationship with the doctoral thesis

The products in this folder are derived from the thesis but should not be confused with the thesis itself.

The thesis establishes the scientific foundation.

The products organize possible academic, technological, educational, and institutional extensions.

The relationship can be summarized as:

```text
doctoral thesis
→ validated scientific framework
→ reproducible repository
→ derivative academic and applied products
```

The thesis remains the core academic document.

The products represent future or parallel outputs derived from the doctoral contribution.

---

## 5. Relationship with repository evidence

Any product in this folder should remain traceable to the corresponding evidence in:

```text
04_RESULTS_COMPLETE/
05_APPENDICES_SUPPORT/
07_REPRODUCIBILITY/
```

For example:

- an article should cite or reuse validated figures, tables, and results;
- a patent note should connect to the formal model, FNRR, or computational architecture;
- a dashboard should be based on documented variables and outputs;
- a presentation should preserve the validated thesis logic;
- a directed thesis should connect to a clear derivative problem;
- a book chapter should distinguish between formal thesis results and broader conceptual interpretation.

This prevents product development from becoming disconnected from the doctoral evidence.

## 6. Interpretation of product maturity

The folders in this section may contain materials at different levels of maturity.

A product may be:

- only planned,
- partially drafted,
- technically outlined,
- under development,
- ready for review,
- submitted,
- published,
- or implemented.

For that reason, the presence of a folder does not imply that the corresponding product is already finished.

Each subfolder should document its own status clearly.

Recommended status labels include:

```text
Planned
Draft
In development
Under review
Submitted
Published
Implemented
Archived
```

## 8. Closure criterion

This folder is considered properly organized when:

1. each product category has a clear purpose,
2. each subfolder contains a README or equivalent description,
3. product maturity is stated honestly,
4. derivative products remain traceable to the thesis evidence,
5. speculative material is separated from validated doctoral results,
6. article and patent materials are organized without overstating their status,
7. presentation material is connected to the thesis structure,
8. directed-thesis material is clearly derivative,
