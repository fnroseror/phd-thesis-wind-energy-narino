\# Article 02 – Concept Note



\## Tentative title

Hybrid physical-statistical forecasting of wind power density in Nariño, Colombia: comparison of classical, machine learning, and deep learning approaches



\## Scientific problem

Forecasting wind-energy-related variables in complex regional systems cannot be treated as a purely algorithmic task. The predictive problem is conditioned by atmospheric variability, nonlinear dependencies, multi-scale temporal structure, and uncertainty. A rigorous comparison of classical, machine learning, and deep learning approaches is required to identify what each modeling family contributes under a physically meaningful framework.



\## Objective

To compare classical, machine learning, and deep learning models for forecasting wind power density in Nariño under a hybrid physical-statistical framework.



\## Expected contribution

This article is expected to contribute:

\- a comparative evaluation of forecasting families under the same regional dataset,

\- evidence on the predictive value of incorporating physical structure into the modeling pipeline,

\- performance analysis against persistence and uncertainty-aware criteria,

\- a rigorous basis for hybrid modeling in regional wind-energy prediction.



\## Supporting thesis/repository evidence

This article is mainly supported by:

\- forecasting outputs from classical models,

\- forecasting outputs from machine learning models,

\- forecasting outputs from deep learning models,

\- model comparison results against persistence,

\- uncertainty-aware evaluation files and PI90-related support.
