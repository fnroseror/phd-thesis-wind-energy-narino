\# Article 01 – Concept Note



\## Tentative title

Physical-statistical characterization of wind power density in Nariño, Colombia, using multi-station meteorological records



\## Scientific problem

Wind resource assessment is often reduced to wind-speed description alone, which can hide the real energetic behavior of the atmospheric system. In regions with strong topographic and climatic variability such as Nariño, Colombia, a more robust characterization requires integrating physical variables and expressing the resource in energetic terms through wind power density.



\## Objective

To develop a physical-statistical characterization of wind power density in Nariño using multi-station meteorological records and to identify the main structural patterns that define the regional wind-energy behavior.



\## Expected contribution

This article is expected to contribute:

\- a regional physical-statistical description of wind power density,

\- a physically grounded interpretation of wind-energy variability,

\- evidence supporting the use of WPD as a more informative target variable than wind speed alone,

\- a structured scientific basis for subsequent predictive and hybrid modeling stages.



\## Supporting thesis/repository evidence

This article is mainly supported by:

\- physical characterization results,

\- descriptive and inferential analysis of meteorological variables,

\- chapter-based figures and tables,

\- WPD-oriented interpretation developed in the doctoral thesis.
