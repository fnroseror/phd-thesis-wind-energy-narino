## 7. Recommended book orientation

The book may be developed under one of three possible orientations.

### 7.1 Scientific book

A technical book focused on:

- wind-energy physics,
- WPD,
- statistical modeling,
- uncertainty,
- FNRR,
- regional energy projection,
- and reproducible computational workflows.

### 7.2 Pedagogical book

A book designed for students, researchers, and early-career scientists, explaining:

- how to think physically about data,
- how to connect equations with real systems,
- how to build a thesis from evidence,
- how uncertainty affects scientific decisions,
- and how regional science can produce original contributions.

### 7.3 Reflective scientific book

A broader book combining:

- the doctoral process,
- physics,
- uncertainty,
- decision-making,
- mental order,
- scientific discipline,
- and the author’s intellectual transformation.

This version must be written carefully so that reflective material does not weaken the scientific rigor of the thesis-derived content.

## 11. Writing principles

The book should follow these principles:

1. **Scientific honesty**  
   Distinguish validated results from interpretation and future work.

2. **Physical clarity**  
   Explain equations as representations of real systems.

3. **Narrative discipline**  
   Avoid excessive conceptual expansion without evidence.

4. **Pedagogical value**  
   Help readers understand not only what was done, but why it matters.

5. **Traceability**  
   Keep the scientific content connected to the thesis and repository.

6. **Conceptual restraint**  
   TDQ, decision, and broader reflections should be clearly framed as extensions.
