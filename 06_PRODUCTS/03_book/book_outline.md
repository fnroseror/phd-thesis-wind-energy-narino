\# Book Outline



\## Tentative title

From wind to decision: physical understanding, uncertainty, and the emergence of a decision-oriented scientific framework



\## General purpose

To develop a book-length work that expands the doctoral thesis into a broader scientific and conceptual narrative, integrating physical interpretation, predictive modeling, uncertainty, usable energy, and the emerging foundations of TDQ.



\## Intended audience

This projected book is oriented toward:

\- researchers interested in wind-energy modeling,

\- readers in applied physics and complex systems,

\- interdisciplinary audiences interested in uncertainty and decision-oriented scientific thinking,

\- advanced academic readers seeking a bridge between rigorous modeling and conceptual interpretation.



\## Conceptual core

The conceptual core of the book is the idea that complex systems should not be understood only in terms of raw potential, but in terms of structured utility, coherence, uncertainty, and decision impact. In the case of the thesis, this logic emerges through wind power density, usable energy estimation, FNRR, and decision-oriented interpretation.



\## Tentative chapter structure



\### Chapter 1. Problem, region, and scientific motivation

Presentation of the regional problem, the scientific relevance of wind-energy forecasting, and the motivation for a physically grounded approach.



\### Chapter 2. Physical understanding of the atmospheric system

Interpretation of the main meteorological variables and their role in the energetic behavior of the wind system.



\### Chapter 3. From wind speed to wind power density

Explanation of why the problem should be expressed in energetic terms and not only as wind-speed variation.



\### Chapter 4. Statistical structure, prediction, and uncertainty

Development of the predictive logic of the thesis, including model comparison and uncertainty-aware interpretation.



\### Chapter 5. Free energy, usable energy, and structural non-regularity

Introduction of the distinction between free energy and usable energy, together with the interpretive role of FNRR.



\### Chapter 6. Toward a decision-oriented scientific framework

Conceptual expansion of the thesis toward a broader decision-based interpretation of physical systems.



\### Chapter 7. Emerging TDQ perspective

Initial formal and conceptual basis for TDQ as an interpretive and decision-oriented framework applicable beyond the wind-energy case.



\### Chapter 8. Scientific projection and future pathways

Discussion of future applications, derivative products, and broader research opportunities.



\## Relation to the thesis and TDQ

The book is conceived as a derivative but broader product.  

The thesis provides the scientific backbone.  

TDQ provides the long-term conceptual expansion.



\## Current development status

Conceptual structure identified.  

Requires future writing and reorganization of thesis-based and theory-based material into book format.



\## Final note

This outline is intended to preserve the direction and identity of the projected book so that the product can be developed coherently in later stages.
